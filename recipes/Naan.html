Naan

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter or ghee
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1/4 cup water (as needed)
  • Optional: minced garlic, chopped cilantro, or nigella seeds for topping
  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the sugar in warm milk. Sprinkle the yeast over the top and let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture, yogurt, melted butter or ghee, and oil.
  3. Mix the ingredients together and knead the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a little flour; if too dry, add a little water.
  4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.
  5. Preheat your oven to its highest setting (usually around 500°F or 260°C) and place a baking sheet or pizza stone inside to heat up.
  6. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into 6-8 equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball and then flatten it into an oval or tear shape using a rolling pin.
  7. If desired, brush the surface of the naan with melted butter or ghee and sprinkle with minced garlic, chopped cilantro, or nigella seeds.
  8. Carefully transfer the rolled naan onto the preheated baking sheet or pizza stone and bake for 2-3 minutes, or until the naan puffs up and gets golden brown spots.
  9. Remove the naan from the oven and brush with additional melted butter or ghee if desired. Serve hot with your favorite curry or dish.
